% NOTE about Bibliography vs References / List of References:
%
% A reference list (often termed just References, and comes after the last
% chapter) usually contains only those sources you cited in-text in your
% manuscript. In contrast a bibliography is usually a full list of the sources
% that you made use of and helped develop the ideas for your study, as well
% as items that influenced your project but were not specifically cited, in
% addition to all the sources that you cited in your actual write-up. The
% bibliography tends to be a much lengthier list and can in some ways be less
% focused than a list of reference. Usually, engineering studies prefer a list
% of references (where as disciplines such as humanities, journalism and social
% sciences commonly use the more extensive bibliography).

\renewcommand{\bibname}{References}         % leave this out if you want "Bibliography"
\bibliography{../Bibliography/Bibliography} %   and
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{References}  % change "References" to "Bibliography" too
